## Teaching advice for Life Skills for Stages 4/5
### Teaching advice for Life Skills Language of number
#### Content
Language of number (Life Skills)
#### Teaching advice
Considerations for teaching Life Skills outcomes and content:
Specific Life Skills outcomes should be selected to meet the particular needs of individual students. Across a year or stage of schooling, students will work towards the number of Life Skills outcomes that are appropriate for their needs.
Teachers can select the content from the focus areas that best addresses the selected outcomes.
The focus areas provide a possible structure for addressing the Life Skills outcomes and content and are suggestions only.
Life Skills outcomes cannot be taught in combination with other outcomes from the same subject.
Students are required to demonstrate achievement of one or more of the Life Skills outcomes.
Key ideas
Students:
identify language which represents number
represent language of number using a quantity of objects
recognise and use number words and ordinal terms.
Key terms
decrease
few
increase
least
more
most
none
number names
ordinal names
zero
integer
digit
ascending
descending
Background knowledge
Opportunity to engage with object permanence
Opportunity to engage with groups and collections of objects of varying amounts in a variety of situations across their day
Recognise one object and more than one object
Making connections
Language of number has many connections to real-world applications and there are opportunities for students to develop knowledge, understanding and skills of this concept across subjects.
Routine problem-solving
Recognise some or none when watching ducks swim in the lake.
Make requests or indicate a preference for activities using terms such as more, less, I want two, I want to go first.
Recognise an order for activities using ordinal terms first, second, third.
Non-routine problem-solving
Follow instructions using language of number to participate in a game or activity.
Create a set of instructions for an activity by applying the language of number and identifying the order of steps using ordinal terms.
Further information in relation to planning, implementing and assessing Life Skills outcomes and content can be found on the NESA website.
